About

Origins

Catfish and the Bottlemen are an indie rock band from Llandudno, Wales in April 2007. Although they are from Wales, only one of
the current members is actually Welsh (See Members Page). They were founded when Van McCann (Lead Singer) moved to
Wales after travelling to Australia with his parents. McCann's parents opened a Bed and Breakfast, which meant McCann had
to sleep in a closet, as there was not enough space. McCann made friends with Stephen and William "Billy" Bibby, whose parents
also owned a B&B. Billy taught McCann how to play guitar, where they wrote some early versions of their current songs. They
were originally known as "The Prestige". Shortly after, Benji Blakeway joined them as the bassist of the band. Bibby also taught
him how to play guitar. Jon Barr, who was a school friend of theirs, joined them on drums as the fourth member of the band.

Replacements

In 2010, Barr was replaced by Robert "Bob" Hall, who was introduced by one of the band's producers at the time. In 2014, Bibby
suddenly left the band in order to look towards his own future. Despite having no plans initially, he founded a new band in 2015,
Billy Bibby and The Wry Smiles. He was replaced by Johnny Bond.

Name Meaning

The bands name comes from one of McCann's memories from his time in Australia. He saw a busker in Sydney who went by the
name of "Catfish the Bottleman". He played beer bottles which were strung on a wire. He adopted the name "Catfish" as he had a
strangely styled beard, similar to a catfish's, when he started busking in 2000.

Albums

They currently have 2 albums out. Their first album, "The Balcony", was released on 15 September 2014. Their second album,
"The Ride", was released on 27 May 2016.

The Balcony

"The Balcony" was Catfish and the Bottlemen's debut album, released in 2014. In the US, it was released on 15 January 2015.
The album cover is an illustration of two headless people feeling each others genitals, drawn by Tim Lahan in 2009. In the UK,
the band reached No. 10 on the UK Albums chart. It also received a Platinum certificate due to selling over 300,000 copies.
In the US, the album reached No. 121 on the US Billboard 200. It also reached No. 8 on the US Independant Albums chart. Reviews
for the album were mixed, with AllMusic and musicOMH giving the album 3 and a half stars out of 5. The Guardian and Q gave the
album 2 out of 5 stars. XS Noize gave the album a 7/10 rating, saying that the album was "an incredibly polished debut album."

The Ride

"The Ride" was Catfish and the Bottlemen's second album, released in 2016. This album's cover has a picture of an alligator
eating its own tail. Van McCann chose this image as he wrote some of the songs in a place called "The Alligator Lounge". He said
"That's where we'd go to smoke and cut loose". In the UK, the album reached No. 1 on the UK albums chart. In the US, it peaked at
28 on the Billboard 200, charting higher than "The Balcony".
